Welcome

Discover the extraordinary story of the Capussi family, whose journey spans two continents and bridges cultures, languages and traditions.

From Milan to Ethiopia

In 1922, Mario Capussi was born in Milan, Italy, to Gaetano Capussi, a skilled aeronautical technician. Father and son would eventually leave Italy for Ethiopia in the 1930s, drawn by the promise of adventure and opportunity in the Horn of Africa. Ethiopia became their second home, where they would build a life that blended Italian heritage with Ethiopian culture.

Rebuilding an Aircraft

Between 1942 and 1943, during the British administration of Ethiopia, Mario and Gaetano undertook a remarkable project: they rebuilt a dismantled aircraft hidden away to prevent confiscation. With limited tools and endless determination, they restored the plane to flight, a feat recognised at a ceremony held at the Imperial Hotel in Addis Ababa.

Protected by Ras Mesfin

In the tumultuous years following the end of Italian rule, many Italians were deported or imprisoned. The Capussi family, however, found protection under Dejazmach Mesfin (later Ras Mesfin), an Ethiopian noble who valued their skills and honour. Mario became a trusted driver, technician and confidant, living with Mesfin’s family and learning their language and customs.

A Marriage of Cultures

During his time in Ethiopia, Mario met Giovanna Kavathas, a woman of Greek-Ethiopian descent. Their marriage united three cultural traditions and gave rise to a multicultural family. The couple’s children, Yolanda and Walter, grew up speaking Italian, Amharic and French in a household that celebrated both Ethiopian and European traditions.

Teacher & Mentor

Mario dedicated much of his life to training young mechanics and aviation specialists. He believed that knowledge should be shared generously. His legacy lives on in the generations of Ethiopians he mentored, who remember him as a principled professional and compassionate teacher.

Friendship with the Brown Condor

Among the notable connections in the Capussi narrative is Mario’s friendship with Colonel John C. Robinson, known as “The Brown Condor,” an African-American aviator who fought for Ethiopia. The two men shared mutual respect and a dedication to aviation. Their bond stands as a symbol of international solidarity and shared values.

Giovanna: The Silent Pillar

Giovanna Kavathas Capussi was the heart of the family. A woman of Greek-Ethiopian heritage, she preserved traditions and instilled values in her children. Her quiet strength and unwavering dedication ensured that the Capussi legacy would endure across generations.

A Living Legacy

Today, the story of the Capussi family lives on through photographs, documents and the memories passed down within the family. Their journey illustrates how cultural exchanges can enrich lives and create enduring bonds between nations.
